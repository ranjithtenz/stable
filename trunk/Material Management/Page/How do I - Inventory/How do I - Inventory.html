1. How do I create Items?

a. To make a Item, click on the Item Link on the Setup Page or click here

2. How do I set my opening stock?

a. In an excel sheet enter 1st Row as - Item Code, Warehouse, Qty, MAR. Under this row mention all your item codes, the warehouse where they are currently stored, the opening quantity and the moving average rate.

b. Save this excel sheet in CSV format.

c. Go to the Stock module and create a new Stock Reconciliation.

d. Fill in the details like Reconciliation Date etc, and go to the second tab called Attachments. Upload the CSV file.

e. Save and submit the Stock Reconciliation. The opening stock is set.

3. What is a Warehouse and what is a “Warehouse Type”?

a. Warehouse is the place where organisation stores its raw materials, finished items, items under process, scraped items etc. Click here to make a Warehouse.

b. Warehouse type is specified on the basis of type of material it stores. For example, warehouse type can be ‘Finished Good’, ‘Raw Material’, ‘Work in Progress’ etc. Click here to make a Warehouse Type.

4. How do I transfer material from one Warehouse to another?

a. To transfer material from one warehouse to other, we need to make Stock Entry.

b. In the new stock entry form opened, select purpose as ‘Material Transfer’, specify name of source warehouse and target warehouse, add details of item to be transferred in ‘Item’ section and save.

c. To make a Stock Entry, click on the Stock Entry Link on the Stock Page or click here

5. Where do I see my stock value?

a. Go to the Stock module.

b. Open Stock Report. (2nd column).

c. You can see the Stock value in the fourth column. Also you can view the total stock value across all your warehouses if you scroll down.

6. What is FIFO valuation and “Moving Average” valuation?

a. FIFO (First in first out) - In case of FIFO, the items which came first will be sold first. The gross profit calculations will consider the purchase rate of items which were bought first. Stock value in this case would be the quantity of items multiplied by the rate at which they were bought.

b. Moving Average - In this case the moving average rate for an item will be calculated as : [(Ra * Qa) + (Rb * Qb)]/(Qa + Qb) where Ra is the incoming rate of the existing quantity Qa of the item and Rb is the incoming rate of the transaction quantity Qb. Stock value will be the total quantity multiplied by the moving average rate.
